RUBY GEMSTONE

INTRODUCTION

Rubies are red in color and are regarded as one of the most popular gems worldwide. They are significantly durable and their color reaches saturation levels like no other. They get their color from the element chromium as it mixes with iron, and the levels can alter the color to have more pink, purple, orange or brown in it, but ultimately it is red that dominates. The mix of chromium and iron is dependent on the geological foundation in which it developed, which is why many rubies are given specific names (like Burmese Ruby, or Thai Ruby); because this is the location of origin and, often, the color represents that location.

Location

MINING RUBY

Rubies can be found all over the world, and the origin impacts the value of the gemstone. The major producer of rubies is Asia, with Myanmar producing the highest valued gems. These gemstones have a fluorescent red colour, and some gems have a near-perfect color of purple-red, with clear saturation and a very dark tone. This beauty is known as ‘pigeon blood red’, and is very rare. Mogok hosts one of the oldest gemstone mines, having been a source of some of the finest rubies for over 1,000 years. They hold their color under any light and are purely a remarkable sight.

Color is the most important element of the gem; not simply because it identifies the type of gem but because it signifies authenticity and rarity. It is what draws in its potential owner; the innate beauty in the color of a gemstone captivates the senses of the people who hold it. The color of a gem is categorized three ways: hue, tone and saturation. Hue identifies the strength of the principal color of the gemstone and includes if there are other colors within the mix. The tone is the brightness (or darkness) of the stone. It also includes whether or not there have been some black or white elements mixed in with the pure color. Saturation refers to the intensity of the color overall. It is the combination of these elements that gives the gemstone its vibrancy, its uniqueness and its undoubted beauty.

Clarity is the identification of brilliance of the gemstone. It involves determining how many (if any) imperfections are within the gem, and the less the better. The fewer fractures or chips a gemstone has, the more brilliant the shine and reflection is, which is what captivates people to want to own one. If you obtain a gemstone high in clarity it is because it has very few imperfections.

The cut of a gemstone refers to the shape in which the gem has been cut and polished by experts in the lab (or lapidary). As a rule, gemstones are either faceted (Flat faces) or non-faceted (rounded faces). While this determines the final shape of the gemstone it also impacts significantly on the brilliance (clarity) of the stone. It is impacted by the number, shape and position of the facets (sides) that are cut into the gemstone. It is also important to note that the cutting of the gemstone can affect the color; some cuts make it lighter while others make it darker. This varies between each gemstone, too, so needs to be taken into considering for pricing.
